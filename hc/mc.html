What is Hacking?

Hacking is the act of identifying and then exploiting weaknesses in a computer system or network, usually to gain unauthorized access to personal or organizational data.

While hacking is not always a malicious activity, the term has mostly negative connotations due to its association with cybercrime.

A hacker is a person who breaks into a computer system. The reasons for hacking can be many: installing malware, stealing or destroying data, disrupting service, and more.

Hacking can also be done for ethical reasons, such as trying to find software vulnerabilities so they can be fixed.
